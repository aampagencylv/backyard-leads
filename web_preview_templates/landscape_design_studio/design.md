# Landscape Design Studio — Design System

> Brand contract for designer-led landscape practices: landscape
> architects, garden designers, botanical-focused studios, regenerative
> design firms, urban habitat designers, master gardeners.
>
> Inspired by earthbounddesigns.com + sydneygrandscapes.com.au. The
> designer is the brand. Their portfolio is the pitch. Phone numbers
> are optional — inquiry forms beat dial-now in this archetype.

## 1. Color philosophy

Deep, restrained, earth-rooted. Anchored on the prospect's brand color
which is typically a deep green, teal, or terracotta. Cream and warm
neutrals do the supporting work — never pure white.

- **Paper** (`#faf7f0`) — body background. Warm cream, like aged paper.
- **Cream-deep** (`#efe7d4`) — section background, panel fill.
- **Ink** (`#0e1b14`) — headings. Deep forest-influenced near-black.
- **Ink soft** (`#3d4a3f`) — body text. Greenish-warm undertone.
- **Mist** (`#d4cdb8`) — hairline borders.
- **Primary** — extracted from logo. Default `#1f3a2e` (deep forest).
- **Primary deep** — auto-darkened for hover states.
- **Accent rust** (`#a4612b`) — used sparingly for project metadata + small details. Botanical-leaning accent.

NO blues unless the prospect's brand is explicitly blue. This archetype
is earth-rooted: greens, terracottas, ochres, cream.

## 2. Typography

Default pairing: **Cormorant Garamond + Inter**, but the per-prospect
DNA may override to playfair_lato or lora_source.

Serif headlines do the work. The body is workhorse sans.

- Hero title (designer's name OR studio name): 64-96px serif, weight 500
- Tagline below: 22-30px serif italic, weight 400-500
- Section headline: 40-56px serif, weight 500
- Body: 17px sans, line-height 1.7
- Eyebrow / project metadata: 11px sans, uppercase, letter-spacing 0.22em
- Caption: 13px sans italic

## 3. Spacing

Generous like luxury (144px section vertical), but not as extreme as
craft_studio. The breath signals taste; cramming signals hurry.

## 4. Layout

- **Hero**: NOT full-bleed photo. A SPLIT layout — text left (designer's name, tagline, eyebrow), large editorial portrait or landscape image right. The viewer sees you AND your work simultaneously. The portrait is taller than wide (4:5).
- **Statement strip**: a single horizontal italic-serif statement below the hero. Like luxury's promise but warmer. E.g. *"Twenty-three years designing gardens that grow with the families who tend them."*
- **Selected projects**: editorial 2-column grid. Each project = large image + project metadata block (project name, location, year, materials, brief). NO portfolio grid of thumbnails — magazine-style writeups.
- **About the designer**: portrait + first-person bio. Standard editorial layout. Bio reads like an interview, not a marketing page.
- **Recognition**: small horizontal strip — publications, awards, professional memberships. Single line of small caps with em-dash separators.
- **Inquiry CTA**: NOT "Get a quote" — "Talk through your project" or "Send us your plans." Soft. Centered cream block.

## 5. Components

### Buttons
- Primary: OUTLINED (1px primary), text primary. Pill-radius (100px) — softer than luxury's sharp corners. Hover invert to filled.
- Letter-spacing 0.08em.
- NEVER "BOOK NOW" or "CALL". This archetype's customers don't panic-call landscape designers.

### Photos
- 4-8px rounded corners. Editorial but not sharp.
- Subtle saturation boost (filter saturate 1.08) — botanical greens render warmer with light boost.
- Hover: gentle box-shadow rise. No scale.

### Project metadata block
- Used in the Selected Projects section.
- Format: project name (24px serif), location (small caps), year, materials list (comma-separated, small italics), 2-sentence description.

### Section dividers
- 1px hairline mist between sections. Almost invisible — signals continuity without interrupting flow.

## 6. Motion

Quiet but present. 0.4s ease on transitions (slower than other templates
— landscape pacing is slow). Section entry: gentle fade-up.

## 7. Voice / tone

- **First-person.** "I started my practice in 2002 after twenty years at..."
- **Botanical specificity.** "We layered native ironwood, manzanita, and toyon" beats "We installed native plants."
- **Time-aware.** Landscape changes over years. Reference that — "ten years in, the oaks are doing the work the lawn used to."
- **Restraint.** "We designed this garden" beats "We TRANSFORMED this BACKYARD into a STUNNING outdoor LIVING space!"
- **NO superlatives.** No "best", "premier", "luxury", "stunning".
- **Hero title = designer's or studio's name.** NOT a service. NOT a tagline. NOT a CTA.
- **Hero tagline = a single italic serif statement.** Soft, considered. Example: *"Gardens for the long arc."* / *"Landscape, considered."*

## 8. Brand guidelines

- The homepage is a PRACTICE STATEMENT, not a service catalog. Goal: an inquiry from someone who already sees the practice's sensibility matches their project.
- Footer: studio name, location, email, single discreet "Designed by {Agency}" line.
- Top banner: italic + cream-bordered, never alarming.

## 9. Anti-patterns

DO NOT generate:

- "Free estimate" or pricing CTAs anywhere
- Phone numbers in hero (in footer only)
- "Why choose us?" sections
- Trust badges (BBB, etc. — landscape designers don't need these)
- "Schedule a service" language
- Star ratings or review widgets
- Stock photos
- "Transform your backyard" or "outdoor living space"
- Multi-column service grids (this template has Selected Projects, not Services)
- Pricing of any kind
- More than ONE primary CTA per scroll
- Gradients
- Drop shadows over 0 8px 24px subtle
- The words "luxury", "premium", "stunning", "transform"
- Exclamation marks
- "Our team" — use first-person OR the designer's name. Plural-pronoun teams are for trade businesses.
